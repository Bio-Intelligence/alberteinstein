Later Life

In his later years, Albert Einstein lived at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, persistently working on a unified field theory while questioning aspects of quantum mechanics. He also emerged as a prominent public thinker, championing civil rights, pacifism , and Zionism and voicing concern over the dangers of nationalism and nuclear weapons. Even with declining health, he remained intellectually engaged, corresponding with scientists, mentoring students, and pondering the broader philosophical and ethical implications of science, leaving a legacy that blended scientific brilliance with a commitment to humanity and global awareness.
