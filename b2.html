His Acheivements

Albert Einstein's work fundamentally transformed physics, primarily through the theories introduced in his "miracle year" of 1905. That year, he published four pivital papers, including his explanation of photoelectric effect-which established that light travels as energy packets called photons and earned him the 1921 Nobel Prize- and the Special Theory of Relativity, which connected space and time and yielded the famous equation, E=mc². A decade later, he completed his most significant achievement, the General Theory of Relativity(1915), which redefined gravity as the curvature of space-time caused by mass. Together, these theories provided the concept for nearly all modern cosmology and quantum mechanics.
